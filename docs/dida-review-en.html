Why the decline — hypotheses

DIDA-specific causes (peers grew)

Because the total and peers grew, the cause lies between DIDA and OMH. Prioritised hypotheses:

1
Mapping / room-type code mismatch Likely

DIDA has persistent Legacy↔Ellis code inconsistency and Single-Use room-type naming issues. Broken mapping shrinks sellable inventory → lower search & conversion. (Internal T19 in progress.)

2
Rate / coverage competitiveness Likely

On the same inventory, Convergent & PKFARE win on rate/coverage and absorb DIDA's volume. Elong also fell (−34%), pointing to a "Chinese low-margin" competitive squeeze.

3
QPS / search coverage limits

DIDA has a history of QPS constraints. Request-rate limits structurally reduce search exposure and conversion.

4
L2B (look-to-book) conversion drop

Possible change in DIDA's front-end exposure/conversion logic. DIDA's internal metrics needed to confirm.

⚠️ Revenue & ADR are unreliable in this dataset (export revenue-field issue), so we used volume metrics only (bookings, room nights). The conclusions (−43% volume, halved share, behind peers) are robust on a volume basis.
Meeting agenda

Questions for DIDA · What to agree

Ask DIDA

  • Any inventory you couldn't sell due to mapping/room-type (incl. Single Use) issues?
  • What do you need from OMH to recover volume?
What OMH will do (if agreed)
  • Finalise mapping alignment / Single-Use decision (T19) → normalise sellable inventory
  • Review Japan (Tokyo/Osaka) rate competitiveness — benchmark vs Convergent & PKFARE
  • Reassess QPS limits (expand search coverage)
  • Discuss joint promotions / target segments (Japan secondary cities, Vietnam)
